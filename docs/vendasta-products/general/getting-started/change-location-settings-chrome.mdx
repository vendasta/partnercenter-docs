# How to change location settings on Google Chrome

Changing your location on Google Chrome is simple. Follow these steps to manually set your location using Google Chrome's developer tools:

1. Click the three-dot icon on the top right of your browser.

2. Go to **More tools > Developer tools.**
   
   :::note
   The original image could not be downloaded. Original source: https://nordvpn.com/wp-content/uploads/blog-geolocation-1.jpg
   :::

3. Choose the **Sensors** tab.
   
   :::note
   The original image could not be downloaded. Original source: https://nordvpn.com/wp-content/uploads/blog-geolocation-2.jpg
   :::

4. If you can't see the tab, click the three-dot icon on the top-right, hover over **More tools,** and click **Sensors.**
   
   :::note
   The original image could not be downloaded. Original source: https://nordvpn.com/wp-content/uploads/blog-geolocation-3.jpg
   :::

5. Click the drop-down menu next to **Location** and choose one of the available options.

6. To add a new location, select **Manage > Add location.**
   
   :::note
   The original image could not be downloaded. Original source: https://nordvpn.com/wp-content/uploads/blog-geolocation-4.jpg
   :::
   
   After you've changed your location, you can test it by entering something in the address or search bar. Scroll down to the bottom of the page, and you'll see what location Google is using for you.
